ISRO's SCATSAT-1 predicts an upcoming cyclone hit in Kerala

ISRO's SCATSAT-1 satellite has provided invaluable data forecasting an impending cyclone threat to the picturesque state of Kerala. This advanced prediction empowers authorities with crucial lead time to implement precautionary measures, reinforcing the significance of cutting-edge technology in disaster management and safeguarding coastal communities.

Probability of earthquake of magnitude 6 in areas of Kutch

Kutch, located in western India, is known for its seismic activity, and the probability of experiencing an earthquake with a magnitude of 6 or higher is a significant concern for the region. Being situated near the tectonic boundary of the Indian and Eurasian plates, Kutch has experienced historic earthquakes, making earthquake preparedness and resilience crucial for its residents and infrastructure.

Heavy rainfall of 1200 mm in the northeast, govt announces evacuation

With a staggering 1200 millimeters of rainfall in the northeastern region, the government has taken swift action to ensure public safety. Announcing a proactive evacuation plan, authorities are prioritizing the well-being of residents in the face of potential flooding and landslides, emphasizing the importance of disaster preparedness and community support.

Cloud Burst in Uttarakhand

A cloud burst, an intense meteorological event, occurred in the picturesque state of Uttarakhand, unleashing torrents of rainwater in a short span of time. This natural phenomenon, while mesmerizing in its own right, has the potential to bring challenges like flash floods and landslides, calling for swift response and preparedness to ensure the safety and resilience of the region's communities.
